# PHASE 6D — SECTION 4 MOBILE PROTOTYPE NOTES

## Purpose

These notes explain why the high-fidelity prototype follows Blueprint V2 and what UX improvement each major design decision is expected to produce.

---

## Major Design Decisions

### 1. Fit Line Elevated Above Title Body

- Why it follows Blueprint:
  - supports the `fit before features` and `recognition before reading` rules
  - aligns with the V2 visual hierarchy placing fit and title at primary attention level
- Blueprint rules satisfied:
  - Visual Hierarchy
  - Decision Flow
  - Card Contract
- Expected UX improvement:
  - faster likely-fit recognition
  - lower hesitation before comparison

### 2. Difference Cue Is Promoted Before Benefit Confirmation

- Why it follows Blueprint:
  - blueprint requires strongest differentiator to support comparison before deep reading
- Blueprint rules satisfied:
  - Decision Flow
  - Information Hierarchy Contract
  - Comparison Model
- Expected UX improvement:
  - faster candidate elimination
  - lower reading burden

### 3. Benefit Row Reduced To Quiet Confirmation Chips

- Why it follows Blueprint:
  - benefits must confirm, not explain fully
  - supporting attention must not compete with primary decision layers
- Blueprint rules satisfied:
  - Visual Priority Rules
  - Card Behavior Rules
- Expected UX improvement:
  - calmer card interiors
  - less cognitive overload on `360` and `375`

### 4. Product Image Remains Strong But Less Dominant

- Why it follows Blueprint:
  - image must support recognition, not dominate meaning
- Blueprint rules satisfied:
  - Image Contract
  - Card Region Intent
- Expected UX improvement:
  - more balanced attention between desirability and understanding

### 5. Price And CTA Form A Clear Commitment Zone

- Why it follows Blueprint:
  - price supports commitment
  - CTA completes the decision
- Blueprint rules satisfied:
  - Card Structural Priority
  - CTA Contract
- Expected UX improvement:
  - cleaner step from understanding to action
  - stronger conversion readiness

### 6. Trust Layer Quieted Below Grid

- Why it follows Blueprint:
  - trust must be supportive and subordinate
- Blueprint rules satisfied:
  - Trust Layer Contract
  - Trust Hierarchy
- Expected UX improvement:
  - reduced post-grid noise
  - better decision recovery before fallback CTA

### 7. Bottom CTA Clearly Reads As Fallback

- Why it follows Blueprint:
  - bottom CTA is for unresolved or consultation-seeking users
- Blueprint rules satisfied:
  - CTA Contract
  - Decision Layer Map
- Expected UX improvement:
  - lower competition with per-card CTAs
  - better role clarity
