# PHASE 6D — SECTION 4 MOBILE PROTOTYPE KNOWN RISKS V3

## Known Prototype Risks

### 1. Smallest-width Support Copy Density Residual Risk

- Risk:
  - `360` width may still feel tight if live copy expands more than expected, even after added breathing room
- Validation needed:
  - overflow
  - readability
  - equal-height stability

### 2. Per-product Image Crop Fairness

- Risk:
  - some packaging may still feel more visually dominant than others depending on image crop
- Validation needed:
  - recognition
  - packaging visibility
  - cross-card consistency

### 3. Old Price Value At Small Widths

- Risk:
  - old price may still add more density than decision value on smaller screens
- Validation needed:
  - whether it remains meaningfully secondary
  - whether it harms scan speed

### 4. Trust To Bottom CTA Rhythm

- Risk:
  - post-grid sequence may still feel slightly compressed if the trust layer and fallback CTA read too close together
- Validation needed:
  - rhythm
  - attention recovery
  - hierarchy separation

### 5. Intro Typography Premium Stability

- Risk:
  - the calmer intro may still need one more pass if the first impression is not yet premium enough under formal review
- Validation needed:
  - first-scan confidence
  - heading stability
  - subtitle comfort

### 6. Responsive Parity Between Smallest And Standard Widths

- Risk:
  - `360` and `375` may still trail `390+` in perceived polish even if they are structurally correct
- Validation needed:
  - visual parity
  - reading comfort
  - decision-flow consistency

### 7. Prototype Fidelity Versus Runtime Asset Behavior

- Risk:
  - this prototype is a visual authority artifact, not a runtime implementation
- Validation needed:
  - ensure prototype conclusions convert cleanly into production implementation constraints later
