# PHASE 6D — SECTION 4 MOBILE INDEPENDENT REVIEW BRIEF

## Purpose

This brief prepares an independent reviewer to evaluate Section 4 Mobile Prototype V2 quickly and consistently.

The reviewer is not being asked to redesign the section.

The reviewer is being asked to determine whether Prototype V2 satisfies Blueprint V2 strongly enough to proceed toward Blueprint Freeze.

---

## What Changed Since Prototype V1

Prototype V2 refined only the previously identified visual-execution blockers.

Key updates:

- intro typography was softened and given more breathing room
- smallest-width cards received more internal breathing room
- Thai fit, difference, and support layers were rebalanced
- trust-to-bottom-CTA spacing rhythm was improved
- the guided decision path was reinforced without changing CTA behavior or product authority

---

## Which Blockers Were Addressed

Prototype V2 specifically addressed:

1. `P1 — Intro Typography`
2. `P1 — 360 / 375 Card Density`
3. `P1 — Thai Readability`
4. `P1 — Guided Decision Flow`

These were treated as presentation-only execution refinements.

No architecture, business logic, commerce, analytics, routing, or CTA behavior was changed.

---

## Which Items Remain Under Validation

Independent reviewers should treat the following as still open for decision:

- whether the intro now feels premium and stable enough
- whether `360` and `375` now feel sufficiently calm and breathable
- whether Thai meaning layers are comfortable enough under real review conditions
- whether old price remains useful at the smallest widths
- whether trust-to-bottom-CTA rhythm is now fully calm
- whether image crop fairness is consistent across all six cards

---

## What Reviewers Must Focus On

Independent review should focus on:

1. first-scan confidence
2. fit recognition speed
3. comparison comfort
4. typography clarity
5. small-width density
6. decision flow integrity
7. CTA role separation
8. post-grid rhythm

The most important question is:

Does Section 4 Mobile now feel easier to choose from before it feels merely cleaner to look at?

---

## Review Guardrails

Independent review must not:

- introduce new UX goals
- expand scope
- reinterpret Blueprint objectives
- request architecture changes
- request CTA-behavior changes
- request commerce changes

The review must stay within Section 4 Mobile presentation authority only.
