# PHASE 6D — SECTION 4 MOBILE LAYOUT AUTHORITY V2

## 1. Executive Summary

Section 4 Mobile is a guided mobile decision layer.

It is not a compact product catalog whose only job is to display inventory.

Its purpose is to help users move through a controlled decision journey:

- discover available options
- recognize likely-fit products quickly
- compare only what matters
- gain enough confidence to choose
- reach LINE with stronger intent

This document defines the visual authority for that journey.

It explains how Section 4 should organize attention, reading flow, decision flow, and content priority so Blueprint V2 can translate those rules into measurable design without reinterpretation.

This document intentionally does not lock measurements.

This document intentionally does not define runtime implementation.

---

## 2. Layout Philosophy

### Recognition Before Reading

The layout must help users identify likely-fit products before they must read deep supporting text.

This means the section should reward fast scanning first and detailed reading second.

### Fit Before Features

Users should understand who a product is for before they process benefit details.

Feature content supports fit recognition.
It must not replace it.

### Confidence Before CTA

The section should build enough certainty before asking for action.

CTA must feel like the next logical step after understanding, not an invitation to act while still uncertain.

### Comparison Before Persuasion

The section must help users compare options before generalized support or consultation messaging becomes prominent.

The decision layer comes first.
The persuasion layer comes second.

### One Dominant Decision At A Time

At any moment, the layout should make one decision task feel primary:

- what this product is
- whether it fits me
- why it differs
- whether I trust it
- whether I want to act

The layout must avoid making all tasks feel equally urgent.

### Premium Through Clarity

Premium does not mean more decoration.

Premium means:

- control
- calm
- visual restraint
- hierarchy that feels deliberate
- confidence without clutter

## 4. Decision Flow

The intended psychological journey is:

Discover

↓

Recognize

↓

Compare

↓

Gain Confidence

↓

Choose

↓

LINE

### Decision Flow Interpretation

#### Discover

Users recognize that multiple relevant products are available.

#### Recognize

Users identify which options may fit their needs.

#### Compare

Users compare only the most relevant candidates instead of studying every detail equally.

#### Gain Confidence

Users feel enough certainty from clarity, difference, price, and trust cues.

#### Choose

Users commit to one product path.

#### LINE

Users enter the conversion flow with intent rather than uncertainty.

## 6. Card Architecture

The card is the primary decision unit.

Every region must have a clear role in the decision journey.

### Image Region

- Purpose: recognition and emotional interest
- Importance: supportive, not dominant
- Relationship: should help users identify the product quickly, but not overpower the meaning layer

### Fit Region

- Purpose: immediate relevance recognition
- Importance: primary
- Relationship: must help users decide whether to continue evaluating the card

### Title Region

- Purpose: product identity anchor
- Importance: primary
- Relationship: works with fit to create fast recognition

### Difference Region

- Purpose: quick distinction from neighboring products
- Importance: high
- Relationship: should help users compare without reading every supporting line

### Benefit Region

- Purpose: confirmation and lightweight support
- Importance: secondary
- Relationship: should reinforce the product story, not carry it alone

### Price Region

- Purpose: commitment cue
- Importance: high
- Relationship: must appear after enough meaning has formed, but before action

### Card CTA Region

- Purpose: product-specific conversion path
- Importance: high
- Relationship: must feel like the immediate next step once confidence exists

### Card Architecture Rules

- The card must move from understanding to commitment.
- The card must not feel like a stack of equally loud components.
- Every region should reduce uncertainty or support action readiness.

## 7. Section Rhythm

Section 4 Mobile should feel controlled, breathable, and mentally recoverable.

### Rhythm Principles

- The intro should create orientation.
- The grid should create structured decision work.
- The trust layer should create reassurance recovery.
- The bottom CTA should create a calm final path.
- The footer should close quietly.

### Rhythm Goal

Users should never feel that the section is shouting every message at once.

The section should alternate:

- attention
- understanding
- reassurance
- action

Premium rhythm means users feel guided, not crowded.

---

## 8. Comparison Model

Users should compare products without reading every word.

The comparison model should work like this:

- see different products
- recognize likely fit categories
- compare the most relevant candidates
- confirm difference with lightweight support cues
- use price and action to finalize choice

### Comparison Rules

- Comparison must rely on visible distinction before deep reading.
- Users should not need to process all benefits across all cards to choose.
- The layout should encourage candidate reduction quickly.
- Comparison should feel faster than the current runtime.

---

## 9. Product Fit Model

The product fit model exists to help users eliminate irrelevant options quickly.

### Fit Model Rules

- Fit should be understandable before feature analysis.
- Fit should reduce hesitation.
- Fit should narrow the choice set mentally.
- Fit should help first-time visitors, not only returning buyers.

### Product Fit Objective

Users should be able to think:

- this is for me
- this is not for me
- this one deserves comparison

without studying the entire card.

## 10. CTA Hierarchy

### Card CTA

- Role: immediate product-specific action
- Psychology: best for users who already recognize likely fit and are ready to move
- Position in hierarchy: primary action inside the decision unit

### Bottom CTA

- Role: fallback consultation and generalized help path
- Psychology: supports users who need assistance or do not want to commit to a specific card yet
- Position in hierarchy: secondary to product-specific choice

### CTA Hierarchy Rules

- Card CTA should complete a product decision.
- Bottom CTA should complete a section-level uncertainty state.
- Users should not feel that both CTAs are asking for the same decision at the same time.

---

## 11. Trust Hierarchy

Trust belongs after comparison because users need product context before reassurance becomes meaningful.

If trust appears too early or too strongly, it competes with the actual choice task.

### Trust Hierarchy Rules

- Trust should confirm safety, not interrupt discovery.
- Trust should lower risk after product evaluation begins.
- Trust should remain subordinate to the product decision layer.
- Trust should support confidence, not create another dense information block.

---

## 12. Responsive Philosophy

The smallest supported mobile width defines the contract.

Larger widths inherit from that contract rather than redefining it.

### Why The Smallest Width Leads

- smallest-width mobile is where cognitive compression is most severe
- if the section works there, larger widths gain comfort rather than needing structural reinvention
- if the section only works at more comfortable widths, the mobile baseline is not stable

### Responsive Philosophy Rules

- mobile structure stays consistent across supported widths
- larger widths may improve breathing room, but not redefine decision order
- the section should scale by comfort, not by conceptual change
